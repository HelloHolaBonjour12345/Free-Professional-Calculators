What Is Body Mass Index?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a widely used measurement that estimates whether an individual has a healthy body weight relative to their height. It is calculated by dividing weight in kilograms by height in meters squared. BMI provides a general indicator of body fatness and is commonly used in medical and public health assessments.

The standard BMI categories are: underweight (below 18.5), normal weight (18.5–24.9), overweight (25–29.9), and obesity (30 or above). These ranges help healthcare professionals evaluate potential health risks associated with body weight.

While BMI is a useful screening tool, it does not directly measure body fat percentage. Factors such as muscle mass, bone density, age and gender are not reflected in BMI calculations. For example, athletes with high muscle mass may have a higher BMI without having excess body fat.

Maintaining a healthy BMI is generally associated with reduced risk of cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes, certain cancers and other chronic conditions. However, BMI should be considered alongside other health indicators such as waist circumference, blood pressure and lifestyle habits.

For a comprehensive evaluation of your health status, consult a qualified healthcare professional who can assess multiple factors beyond BMI alone.
